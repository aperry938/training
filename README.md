## Non-negotiables

1. **6–8 hr between AM and PM sessions.** Research-backed: separates the concurrent-training interference effect.
2. **Sleep 8+ hours.** Two-a-days don't work on 6.
3. **Weekly massage anchor on Thursday.** Recovery is training.
4. **Handstand skill goes first, fresh.** Don't put it after full-body fatigue.
5. **Every 6–8 weeks, take a deload week.** Cut volume ~50%, keep intensity, extra yin + massage.

## Principles (the why)

- **Frequency > volume for skill.** 5 min daily handstand beats 1 × 30 min per week. CNS learns through repetition, not load.
- **Running and heavy legs don't share a day.** Tue pull → Tue run (upper only in AM, fine). Wed legs → Sat run (72 hr gap).
- **Lean + aesthetic ≠ low volume.** You get there via protein, sleep, and conditioning — not by undertraining. You'll lift heavy. You just won't squat for maximal spinal load.
- **Vegan macros carry more weight for combined training.** 2.0 g/kg protein is the floor, not the ceiling. Split across 4–5 doses.
- **Heat, not altitude.** Chiang Mai is ~310 m — no hypoxic tailwind. Heat and humidity are what spike your HR, so run at dawn and let pace come slowly. The Doi Suthep climbs are your only real elevation.

## When something breaks

- **Sleep < 7 hrs two nights in a row** → skip that day's PM session, keep AM light.
- **Resting HR up 5+ bpm for 3+ days** → deload this week.
- **Bar speed slows at same load** → deload soon, or your technique is drifting.
- **Handstand hold time drops** → CNS fatigue (not skill loss) — more recovery, less novelty.
- **Knee / lower back complains on Wed legs** → sub Zercher squat or goblet squat for front squat, skip Bulgarian split squat for a week, check hip mobility first.

## Adjusting for Chiang Mai life

- **Three seasons, not one.** Cool/dry Nov–Feb is prime training weather. Hot season Mar–May (up to ~40°C) — train at dawn, hydrate hard. Rainy/green Jun–Oct is humid with afternoon storms, so run in the morning.
- **Burning season Feb–Apr.** Agricultural + forest-fire smoke pushes PM2.5 to 3–5× the WHO limit (Chiang Mai was the world's most polluted city in March 2026). You're keeping runs outdoors — so check IQAir daily, keep an N95 handy, and move to the Pump 2.0 treadmill on hazardous days.
- **Pump Fitness 2.0** (Chiang Mai Gate) is your home gym for the AM compounds. If it has no trap bar, sub a conventional/sumo deadlift or hex-bar — front squat + Bulgarian split squat already cover the squat pattern.
- **Massage capital.** Even cheaper and more abundant than before — street Thai massage ~250–300฿/hr, or a spa like Fah Lanna (Nimman) for deep tissue. Keep the Thursday anchor.
- **Don't drink the tap water.** Filtered or bottled only; in the heat you'll want 4–5 L/day.
