# Running Plan — Chiang Mai 8-Week Base Build

You've lifted for years but you're new to running. The goal of Weeks 1–8 is **aerobic base** — not speed, not mileage. We build the engine before we stress it.

## Why Zone 2 for 8 weeks

- Zone 2 (60–70% max HR) develops mitochondrial density, capillary network, and fat oxidation. These are the foundations that make every higher zone possible.
- Improvements in pace-at-the-same-HR take **2–3 months** of consistent Zone 2. Don't panic that you're slow — you're supposed to be.
- Running creates more interference with strength training than cycling. Keeping early weeks short and low-intensity minimizes that cost.

## Heart rate math

- **Max HR (Tanaka estimate):** 208 − 0.7 × age. For age 30 that's ~187. For 40 ~180. Use a watch that reads HR or learn your breathing at effort (conversational pace = Zone 2).
- **Zone 2 range:** roughly 60–70% of max. For 180 max → 108–126 bpm. For 187 max → 112–131 bpm.
- **Chiang Mai heat adjustment:** expect HR 5–10 bpm higher at the same effort in the heat and humidity. Don't fight it — run at dawn, run slower, and keep HR in zone.

If you don't have a HR monitor, the talk test works: you should be able to speak in full sentences without panting. If you can sing, you're below Zone 2. If you can only grunt words, you're above.

## The 8-week progression

| Week | Tuesday (Ang Kaew · CMU reservoir) | Saturday (Doi Suthep trail) | Weekly min |
|------|----------------------------------|----------------------------|-----------|
| **1** | 25 min Z2 — walk breaks OK | 30 min Z2 | 55 |
| **2** | 25 min Z2 | 35 min Z2 | 60 |
| **3** | 30 min Z2 | 40 min Z2 | 70 |
| **4** | 30 min Z2 | 45 min Z2 | 75 |
| **5** | 35 min Z2 | 50 min Z2 + 1 × 5 min Z3 | 90 |
| **6** | 35 min Z2 | 55 min Z2 + 1 × 5 min Z3 | 95 |
| **7** | 40 min Z2 | 60 min Z2 + 2 × 5 min Z3 | 105 |
| **8** | 40 min Z2 | 70–75 min Z2 + 2 × 5 min Z3 | 115 |

**Z3 = tempo** — comfortably hard, you can say short phrases but not full sentences. Insert mid-run, not at the start or end.

After Week 8 you'll have a genuine aerobic base. From there options open up: marathon training, trail ultras (Doi Inthanon / Thailand by UTMB beckons), or just maintaining and progressing into some Z4/Z5 work. We'll re-plan then.

## Chiang Mai routes

### Ang Kaew Reservoir — CMU (primary easy route)
- **~1 km reservoir loop · flat · shaded**
- Combine with the Huay Kaew Arboretum paths and CMU's jogging tracks to stack several shaded kilometres
- Default Tuesday Zone 2 route — minutes from Nimman/Suthep
- Popular with students and runners; calm and cool at dawn

### Doi Suthep — Monk's Trail / Wat Pha Lat (primary long route)
- **Lower leg:** Monk's (Pilgrim's) Trail to Wat Pha Lat — **4.3 km / +222 m**, jungle singletrack marked with orange cloth
- **Full climb:** continue up to the chedi at **Wat Phra That Doi Suthep (1,073 m)** — **~7.5 km / +620 m** total
- Dirt, roots, and worn temple steps — your Saturday long climb once you're past Week 3
- Start at the CMU/zoo trailhead; bring water, the jungle is humid

### Huay Tung Tao + Canal Road (flat long alternative)
- **3.5 km lake perimeter loop** + a **5 km paved Canal Rd cycle path** to the north
- Flat and open — good when you want distance without the climb, or to spare the legs
- Less shade, so go early

### Old City moat loop (urban / travel default)
- **~7 km** flat circuit, lit at night, dead central
- Handy and always available, but traffic-y with an uneven sidewalk — watch your footing
- Nong Buak Haad Park (SW corner) is a quiet spot for strides or mobility

### Thailand by UTMB · Doi Inthanon (long-term goal)
- 20K / 50K / 100K / 100-mile around **Doi Inthanon**, Thailand's highest mountain (route spans 400–2,100 m)
- Held late Nov / early Dec — i.e. cool, clear season
- Don't touch before Week 12 of consistent running — a Q4 target if the pull of ultra trails grabs you

## Equipment

- **Road shoes** (Ang Kaew / moat): any cushioned trainer — Brooks Ghost, Asics Cumulus, Nike Pegasus all fine
- **Trail shoes** (Doi Suthep): lugged sole mandatory — Altra Lone Peak, Salomon Sense Ride, Saucony Peregrine. Dirt, roots, and worn temple steps make road shoes dangerous
- **HR monitor**: optional but useful. Watch (Garmin/Coros) or chest strap
- **Water**: 500 ml handheld for runs >45 min — non-negotiable in the heat; the Doi Suthep trail has no reliable water
- **N95 mask**: burning-season essential (Feb–Apr) — check IQAir before any outdoor run

## Race / event options in Thailand (if you want targets)

- **HOKA Chiang Mai Thailand by UTMB** (late Nov / early Dec) — 20K to 100-mile around Doi Inthanon · the iconic Northern Thailand mountain ultra
- **Chiang Mai Marathon** (Muang Thai Chiang Mai Marathon, usually December) — 10K / half / full · cool-season road race
- **CNX trail-running community + park runs** — year-round informal options around Huay Tung Tao and Doi Suthep

Race entries are cheap by Western standards. Excellent value — and note the big mountain events fall in cool season, not burning season.

## Integration with lifting

| Day | Order | Notes |
|-----|-------|-------|
| **Tue** | AM pull → PM run | Upper body lifted in AM, legs fresh in PM. Minimum 6 hr gap. |
| **Wed** | Legs AM — no run | Full recovery window for Sat trail run |
| **Sat** | Trail long run AM only | No PM session. Legs get Sunday off too. |

**Red flags that you're over-cooking concurrent training:**
- Run HR elevated 10+ bpm at same pace for multiple sessions
- Lifting bar speed slow on Wed legs despite adequate sleep
- Persistent low-grade soreness that doesn't clear in 48 hrs

Response: cut one run to 20 min walk, extend Thursday mobility.

## Heat, humidity & air quality — the Chiang Mai factors

Chiang Mai sits at only ~310 m, so altitude is a non-factor (unlike Đà Lạt's 1,500 m). What shapes your running here is **heat, humidity, and seasonal air quality.**

**Heat & humidity:**
- Hot season (Mar–May) hits ~40°C, and even cool-season afternoons are warm. Heat drives HR up at the same pace, drains you faster, and raises fluid + electrolyte loss.
- **Run at dawn** — cooler, cleaner, quieter. Slow down to keep HR in Zone 2; pace will look "wrong" until you acclimatise (~2–3 weeks).
- Hydrate before, during (>45 min), and after; add electrolytes on every run.

**Air quality (burning season, Feb–Apr):**
- Agricultural and forest-fire smoke pushes PM2.5 to **3–5× the WHO limit**, peaking near 400 µg/m³ — Chiang Mai has topped the world's-most-polluted-city rankings in March.
- You're keeping the plan outdoors, so manage it actively: **check IQAir every morning**, wear an N95 if you run, and shift to the **Pump 2.0 treadmill** when AQI is hazardous. A skipped outdoor run in March is cheaper than the lung cost.

**Elevation, where it exists:**
- Only the climbs give you any — Doi Suthep's chedi at 1,073 m, Doi Pui at 1,676 m, Doi Inthanon at 2,565 m. The city itself offers no hypoxic stimulus, so train the hills for the hills, not for altitude adaptation.
