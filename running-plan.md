# Running Plan — Đà Lạt 8-Week Base Build

You've lifted for years but you're new to running. The goal of Weeks 1–8 is **aerobic base** — not speed, not mileage. We build the engine before we stress it.

## Why Zone 2 for 8 weeks

- Zone 2 (60–70% max HR) develops mitochondrial density, capillary network, and fat oxidation. These are the foundations that make every higher zone possible.
- Improvements in pace-at-the-same-HR take **2–3 months** of consistent Zone 2. Don't panic that you're slow — you're supposed to be.
- Running creates more interference with strength training than cycling. Keeping early weeks short and low-intensity minimizes that cost.

## Heart rate math

- **Max HR (Tanaka estimate):** 208 − 0.7 × age. For age 30 that's ~187. For 40 ~180. Use a watch that reads HR or learn your breathing at effort (conversational pace = Zone 2).
- **Zone 2 range:** roughly 60–70% of max. For 180 max → 108–126 bpm. For 187 max → 112–131 bpm.
- **Đà Lạt altitude adjustment:** expect HR 5–10 bpm higher at the same effort for the first 2–3 weeks. Don't fight it — run slower to keep HR in zone.

If you don't have a HR monitor, the talk test works: you should be able to speak in full sentences without panting. If you can sing, you're below Zone 2. If you can only grunt words, you're above.

## The 8-week progression

| Week | Tuesday (Xuân Hương · 5 km loop) | Saturday (Tuyên Lâm trail) | Weekly min |
|------|----------------------------------|----------------------------|-----------|
| **1** | 25 min Z2 — walk breaks OK | 30 min Z2 | 55 |
| **2** | 25 min Z2 | 35 min Z2 | 60 |
| **3** | 30 min Z2 | 40 min Z2 | 70 |
| **4** | 30 min Z2 | 45 min Z2 | 75 |
| **5** | 35 min Z2 | 50 min Z2 + 1 × 5 min Z3 | 90 |
| **6** | 35 min Z2 | 55 min Z2 + 1 × 5 min Z3 | 95 |
| **7** | 40 min Z2 | 60 min Z2 + 2 × 5 min Z3 | 105 |
| **8** | 40 min Z2 | 70–75 min Z2 + 2 × 5 min Z3 | 115 |

**Z3 = tempo** — comfortably hard, you can say short phrases but not full sentences. Insert mid-run, not at the start or end.

After Week 8 you'll have a genuine aerobic base. From there options open up: marathon training, trail ultras (La An beckons), or just maintaining and progressing into some Z4/Z5 work. We'll re-plan then.

## Đà Lạt routes

### Xuân Hương Lake Circuit
- **5 km paved loop · flat · lit at night**
- 4.4★ popular route, safe, runners and walkers always present
- Default Tuesday Zone 2 route
- Can run any time — dawn loops are cinematic

### Tuyên Lâm Lake — West Trail (primary long route)
- **10.3 km / 6.4 mi out-and-back**
- **+1,108 ft elevation** · pine forest singletrack
- Avg 2 hr 59 min pace (hiking); for easy run target ~70–90 min
- Saturday long run default once you're past Week 3
- Bring water — the pine smell makes it easy to forget how far you've gone

### Tuyên Lâm full perimeter (~7 km variant)
- Lake perimeter mixed terrain
- Good intermediate before committing to the 10 km west trail
- Use for Week 4–5 transitions

### La An Ultra Trail (long-term goal)
- **36 km / +5,045 ft** · the biggest trail in the Lâm Đồng region
- Don't touch before Week 12 of consistent running
- Q3/Q4 2026 target if the pull of ultra trails grabs you

## Equipment

- **Road shoes** (Xuân Hương): any cushioned trainer — Brooks Ghost, Asics Cumulus, Nike Pegasus all fine
- **Trail shoes** (Tuyên Lâm): lugged sole mandatory — Altra Lone Peak, Salomon Sense Ride, Saucony Peregrine. Pine needles + roots + occasional mud make road shoes dangerous
- **HR monitor**: optional but useful. Watch (Garmin/Coros) or chest strap
- **Water**: 500 ml handheld for runs >45 min. Tuyên Lâm has no reliable water sources
- **Headlamp**: if running Tuyên Lâm early morning or toward dusk — pine canopy gets dark fast

## Race / event options in Vietnam (if you want targets)

- **Đà Lạt Ultra Trail** (usually Mar/Apr) — 10 km to 100 km distances, based out of Tuyên Lâm area
- **Vietnam Mountain Marathon** (Sapa, September) — iconic route
- **Dalat Marathon** (annual, usually Apr/May) — 5 km / half / full — flat Xuân Hương circuit-based

Race entries cost <$50 USD typically. Excellent value for the experience.

## Integration with lifting

| Day | Order | Notes |
|-----|-------|-------|
| **Tue** | AM pull → PM run | Upper body lifted in AM, legs fresh in PM. Minimum 6 hr gap. |
| **Wed** | Legs AM — no run | Full recovery window for Sat trail run |
| **Sat** | Trail long run AM only | No PM session. Legs get Sunday off too. |

**Red flags that you're over-cooking concurrent training:**
- Run HR elevated 10+ bpm at same pace for multiple sessions
- Lifting bar speed slow on Wed legs despite adequate sleep
- Persistent low-grade soreness that doesn't clear in 48 hrs

Response: cut one run to 20 min walk, extend Thursday mobility.

## Altitude training — what it does and doesn't do

Đà Lạt sits at ~1,500 m. Research on altitude training:

- **Above 600 m:** measurable performance decrement at sub-max efforts
- **Above 2,500 m:** meaningful EPO / hemoglobin increase after 3–4 weeks
- **1,500 m (you):** modest hypoxic stimulus — enough that you'll feel it in weeks 1–2, not enough for dramatic hematological adaptations

**What this means for you:**
- When you travel to sea level (Đà Nẵng transit, HCMC, anywhere) — everything feels easier for 1–2 weeks
- Race PRs at altitude aren't realistic — factor in 5–8% slower pace vs sea level
- Long-term, living at 1,500 m is a small tailwind for aerobic fitness
