# Nutrition — Vegan, Concurrent Training, Đà Lạt

Built on your existing macro plan (`vegan-nutrition-&-exercise.docx`) with adjustments for adding running and emphasizing lean-aesthetic.

## Daily target (75 kg bodyweight assumed)

| Macro | Target | Ratio | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|-------|
| **Calories** | 3,400–3,700 kcal | — | Maintenance → slight surplus. Drop to 3,200 if leaning out. |
| **Protein** | 145–155 g | ~2.0 g/kg | **Up from your previous 132 g.** Vegan + concurrent training warrants 1.8–2.2 g/kg. |
| **Carbs** | 500–560 g | ~7 g/kg | Keep high. Running + lifting = high glycogen demand. |
| **Fat** | 80–90 g | ~22% | Room for avocado, nuts, oils — don't fear fat. |

## Why bump protein

Research consensus (see sources): vegan athletes doing concurrent training need **10–20% more protein than omnivore equivalents** because of:

1. Lower digestibility of plant protein (DIAAS scores)
2. Lower leucine content per gram — less mTOR stimulation per dose
3. Higher fiber slowing absorption

Your current 132 g at 75 kg = 1.76 g/kg. That's fine for pure strength or pure endurance but thin for **both** at the same time with skill work layered on.

## Protein distribution — 4–5 doses / day

Muscle protein synthesis caps at roughly 0.4–0.6 g/kg per meal (~30–40 g for you), and MPS rates stay elevated for ~3 hours. So hitting 150 g in 2 meals is less effective than spreading across 4–5.

| Timing | Target | Source ideas |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| Meal 1 (split #1) | 25–30 g | oats + hemp + chia + flax mix |
| Meal 1 (split #2, ~90 min later) | 25–30 g | same mix, lighter portion |
| Meal 2 | 35–40 g | lentils + rice + tofu add-on |
| Post-workout shake | 25–30 g | pea/soy isolate + banana |
| Meal 3 (evening) | 35–40 g | restaurant or home lentil/rice + tempeh |

## Supplements

| Supplement | Dose | Timing | Why |
|-----------|------|--------|-----|
| **Caffeine** | 100–200 mg | 30 min pre-AM lift | Performance, fine to keep |
| **Creatine monohydrate** | 5 g | Daily (any time) | Only proven hypertrophy + strength ergogenic, ~10% boost |
| **B12 (methyl or cyano)** | 250 µg daily or 2,000 µg weekly | With meal | **Non-negotiable** for vegans — no reliable plant source |
| **Algal DHA + EPA** | 500 mg combined | Daily w/ meal | Offsets omega-6 dominance of plant diet; flax converts poorly |
| **Vitamin D3 (vegan lichen)** | 1,000–2,000 IU | Daily | Đà Lạt sun is real but deficiency is common in anyone who works indoors |
| **Pea / soy isolate** | 30 g | Post-workout | Shake delivery to hit protein distribution target |
| **Electrolytes** (Na, K, Mg) | 1 serving | Run days, esp. trail | Sweating + altitude = salt loss |

**Skip/optional:**
- Multivitamin: redundant if diet is varied + above supplements taken
- BCAA: redundant if hitting protein target
- Glutamine: no benefit shown at your training level
- Pre-workout blends: caffeine + creatine gets you 90% of the effect

## Two-a-day meal timing

| Time | What | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| ~6:30 AM | Wake, water, black coffee | Don't eat heavy — blood goes to digestion, not lift |
| ~7:30 AM | **AM Lift** (fasted or mini breakfast) | If hungry: banana + 15 g protein |
| ~9:00 AM | Post-lift shake (30 g pea/soy + banana) | Within 30 min of last set |
| ~10:30 AM | Meal 1 · part 1 (oats + seeds) | Refuel glycogen + second protein dose |
| ~12:30 PM | Meal 1 · part 2 | Third protein dose |
| ~3:00 PM | Meal 2 (lentils + rice + tofu/avo) | Main fueling for PM session |
| ~5:30 PM | Optional pre-PM snack: rice cake + PB | Only if PM is run or heavy skill |
| ~6:00 PM | **PM session** (run / skill / yoga) | |
| ~7:30 PM | Meal 3 (restaurant or home) | Recovery + satiety — no problem with carbs here |
| ~10:00 PM | Sleep | 8+ hrs |

Shift wall-clock times to match your rhythm; the ratios hold.

## Hydration

- **Baseline:** 3.5–4.5 L water daily at your bodyweight + activity level
- **Run days:** +500 ml pre-run + electrolyte drink during if >45 min
- **Signals you're under-hydrated:** dark urine, afternoon headache, low bar speed, cotton-mouth during skill work

Đà Lạt water is cold mountain water and a pleasure to drink. No excuse.

## Pre-competition / lean-out protocol (future)

If you ever want to cut for a photo/goal:

- Drop 300–400 kcal/day (mostly carbs, keep protein at 2.2 g/kg)
- Keep training volume, reduce intensity slightly
- 8–12 weeks max before diet fatigue sets in
- Refeed day once a week (high carb, same protein, moderate fat)

For now you're in **build mode, lean build**. Not cutting.
