# Skill Progressions — Handstand, Arm Balance, Inversion Strength

Three parallel tracks. Work them in rotation. Frequency over volume — 5–10 min most days beats a single 45-min Sunday session.

## The three tracks

| Track | Goal | Mechanism |
|-------|------|-----------|
| **Handstand balance** | Freestanding 60+ s → press to handstand from L-sit | CNS + fine motor + proprioception |
| **Arm balance** | 19-asana flow clean through → bakasana→handstand | Shoulder stability + core + rotational control |
| **Pike → HSPU** | Freestanding handstand push-up | Raw pressing strength |

You're likely near Level 3–4 on Handstand (given the press-to-handstand in your 19-asana flow) and at Step 2–3 on Arm Balance (flying crow is in your flow). HSPU track we're starting from Gate 1–2.

## Why frequency beats volume

From the research (Kyle Weiger, calisthenics literature): the nervous system consolidates skill during rest — so 4 × 20-min sessions produce more learning than 1 × 80-min marathon. Your CNS needs repetition more than it needs fatigue.

Practical implication: stick a wall handstand hold after your daily mobility reset, even on rest days. 3 × 30 s is enough.

## Track 1 · Handstand Balance

### Level 1 · Wall handstand, chest to wall
**Criterion to progress:** 60 s hold × 3 clean sets, shoulders stacked, belly button to spine, no banana-back.
**Drill:** Kick up facing the wall, pike-in until chest touches. Pinch shoulders into ears. Squeeze glutes.

### Level 2 · Wall handstand, back to wall
**Criterion:** 60 s × 3 with only heel taps on wall. Begin shifting weight forward to fingers.
**Drill:** After your wall-facing holds, flip around, kick up back-to-wall, hold. Practice taking heels off for 2–3 s.

### Level 3 · Freestanding holds
**Criterion:** Accumulate 30 s freestanding across attempts. 10–15 kick-up attempts per session.
**Drill:** Fingers press firmly, balance via finger pressure not hip wobble. Stack toes over hips over shoulders over hands.

### Level 4 · Press to handstand from pike
**Criterion:** 3 clean reps unassisted. Straddle press from wide-leg fold, both legs rise together.
**Drill:** Start facing a wall, fingers 6 in away. Pike up, push floor down through shoulders. Compress toes together before lifting.

### Level 5 · Press to handstand from L-sit
**Criterion:** 2 clean reps. From L-sit, compress into pike, straddle up, legs together at top.
**Drill:** Your current target. Requires L-sit 15+ s minimum. Work L-sit compression on parallettes or blocks.

### Level 6 · One-arm wall progression
**Criterion:** Patience — months past Level 5. Two-finger lift → three fingers → full one-arm wall-assisted → freestanding.
**Drill:** Only introduce this when Level 5 is bulletproof. Injury risk is high if skipped.

## Track 2 · Arm Balance Ladder

### Base · Bakasana (crow)
**Criterion:** 30 s clean hold, knees pinned on triceps (not armpits), gaze forward not down.
**Drill:** Block under forehead for confidence. Lift hips high, squeeze everything.

### Step 1 · Side crow (parsva bakasana)
**Criterion:** 15 s each side. Knees stacked on one tricep, opposite shoulder must support weight.
**Drill:** Start from malasana (deep squat), twist, plant both palms, lean forward until feet lift.

### Step 2 · Flying crow (eka pada galavasana)
**Criterion:** 5 s hold × 5 each side. Back leg straight.
**Drill:** From standing, figure-4 one ankle on opposite thigh, fold, plant hands, hook shin on tricep, lift back leg.

### Step 3 · Eka pada side crow
**Criterion:** 5 attempts with momentary hold. Side crow with one leg extended.
**Drill:** Requires strong obliques + shoulder stability. Film yourself — symmetry reveals weak side.

### Step 4 · Mayurasana (peacock)
**Criterion:** 10 s hold, legs off floor, body parallel to ground.
**Drill:** Novelty strength. Fingers point toward feet, elbows tight into solar plexus, lean forward until legs lift. Wrist prep essential.

### Step 5 · Flying crow → handstand
**Criterion:** Float from flying crow through chaturanga arms, press to handstand. Your current 19-asana goal.
**Drill:** Requires Track 1 Level 4 (press to handstand) mastered.

## Track 3 · Pike → HSPU Strength

Pure pressing strength. Parallel track — don't replace the skill work, add to it.

### Gate 1 · Tricep push-up
**Criterion:** 10–12 clean reps. Elbows tight to ribs, not flaring.
**Programming:** 3–4 × 8 at end of Upper Push or Upper Volume day.

### Gate 2 · Pike hold (feet on box)
**Criterion:** 30–45 s, hips stacked vertically over shoulders.
**Drill:** Box/bench under feet, walk hands in until hips are over shoulders. Look at knees.

### Gate 3 · Pike push-up (box)
**Criterion:** 3–5 sets × 5 clean reps. Head gently touches floor, press back up.
**Programming:** Replace overhead press on Fri Upper Volume once the standard is met.

### Gate 4 · Deficit pike push-up
**Criterion:** 3 × 5. Hands elevated on parallettes/blocks so head drops below hand line.
**Drill:** Progression overload for Gate 3. Slow eccentric.

### Gate 5 · Wall HSPU (chest to wall, head to floor)
**Criterion:** 3 × 3 clean reps.
**Drill:** Mat under head for confidence. Lower controlled, press explosively.

### Gate 6 · Freestanding HSPU
**Criterion:** One clean rep from freestanding handstand.
**Drill:** Requires Track 1 Level 3+ solid. This is a 6–18 month goal from Gate 5 for most people.

## Weekly integration (how these fit the protocol)

| Session | Skills focus |
|---------|--------------|
| **Mon PM · Handstand + core** | Track 1 · Levels 3–5 work (press practice) + hollow body, L-sit, dragon flag |
| **Wed PM · Vinyasa** | Track 2 · full 19-asana flow with Track 1 holds woven in |
| **Fri PM · Arm balance** | Track 2 · Step 2–5 deliberate practice · Track 3 Gate 3–4 pike work |
| **Daily 2–3 min** | Track 1 · wall handstand holds, one set after mobility |
| **Wed mornings (after leg day before yoga) — optional** | Track 3 pike or tricep push-up set, low volume |

**Never on the same day:** Track 1 + Track 3 at high intensity. Either balance or strength, not both — they compete for the same shoulder/elbow tissue.

## Wrist prep · non-negotiable before skill days

5 min routine:

1. Palm stretch (fingers up, gentle press) — 30 s
2. Palm stretch (fingers down) — 30 s
3. Wrist rotations — 20 each direction
4. Knuckle push-ups (on soft surface) — 10
5. Floor press, fingertips-only push-up — 5
6. Kneeling wrist extension stretch — 60 s

Wrist injuries from handstand work are 95% preventable with consistent prep. Don't skip.

## What "being good at handstands" looks like long-term

- **1 min** freestanding hold — proficient, can demo
- **3 min** — strong hobbyist
- **Press to handstand from L-sit** — the mark of real practice (a few thousand reps)
- **One-arm handstand** — 1,000+ hours of deliberate work
- **Freestanding HSPU** — pressing strength is rarer than balance among amateurs; combining both is uncommon

Your current flow (19 asanas with chin stand, peacock, flying crow, press to handstand) is advanced. You're not at zero. You're optimizing from a high base.
